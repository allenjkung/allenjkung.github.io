Ethical Hacking

Why Create This Website?

The question I am answering is what is the line that defines ethical hacking? Ethical hacking is to used describe hacking performed by a company or individual to help indentify potential threats on a computer or network[1]. The infomation that is obtained from hacking is then used to better the organization's security. The conflict with defining the line of ethical hacking is that there is a grey area. Hackers can be people who prey on unsupecting victims, people who defend the internet from those with malicious intent, people who are vigilantes or curious minds. There needs to be a line that defines ethical hacking because there are people who are wrongly accused or punished for hacking ethically and people who are accused for actions that are not defined as hacking.

If it is possible to make ethical hacking black and white, it will allow case to case concerns about hacking more clear and people will be able to experiment and research with hacking without the fear of accusation.


General Overview of The CFAA

This research question will concentrate on the United States and its laws and terms about ethical hacking. Computer Fraud and Abuse Act(CFAA) is the main law in the United States that addresses hacking. Since the day of its creation, there has been a total of 8 reforms [2].

Types of Offenses

1. Obtaining national security information
2. Compromising confidentiality
3. Trespassing goverment computer
4. Accessing to defruad and obtain value
5. Damaging computer or information
6. Trafficking passwords
7. Threatening to damage a computer

Types of Access

1. Without Access (without the user's permission)
2. Exceeds Authorization (accessing beyond what the user gave permisson to)

Martin McKeay is a security expert who works for Akamai Technologies. McKeay works under the Information Secuirty Industry in 1997. He started his career in security by blogging and then hosting a podcast in 2003. He used blogging to test his ideas of security and to improve his theories.

His concern with the CFAA effects on ethical hacking is that the CFAA has the ability to accuse people who downloaded a file on the internet. The problem is that downloading a file is not hacking.

The interview with McKeay shows the flaws in the CFAA and that defining the line for ethical hacking is not possible because of how the large the grey is.

The Future

After everything, the conflict with defining the line of ethical hacking is that the grey area is too large to be defined as black and white. The range of people that can be accused under the CFAA is too general and broad. To better the law that surrounds the CFAA, there needs to be a reform where judgement is based off the integrity of the judge and not the words of the bill. The severity of the punishment needs to be decided case by case so that the punishment is just. For every era, technology will constantly expand and change so the laws that surround ethical hacking needs to adapt and change as well.
